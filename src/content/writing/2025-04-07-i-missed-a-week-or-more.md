---
title: "I Missed a Week (or More)"
date: "2025-04-07"
---

I started a new full-time role, which temporarily shifted my priorities away from my daily writing commitment.

In the past, missing even one self-imposed deadline would typically signal the end of a project. I'd abandon it quietly, weighed down by shame over breaking my perfect streak. This time, however, feels different.

Rather than viewing the gap as failure, I'm reframing it as part of my authentic journey. Posting every day isn't the goal—the disciplined pursuit of thinking in public, wrestling with ideas, and being genuinely open about the messiness of growth is.

The core shift involves transparency. Instead of hiding the absence, I chose to acknowledge it openly—recognizing that growth emerges through honest communication about struggles, not through pretending perfection never breaks. I return to writing with renewed purpose, focusing less on consistency metrics and more on genuine intellectual development.
